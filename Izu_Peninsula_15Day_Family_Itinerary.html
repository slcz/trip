✈️ Flights — US to Tokyo

💡 August Savings Tip: August is one of the cheapest months to fly US→Tokyo. The best week is Aug 31, but mid-August still averages ~$800–950/person from West Coast hubs. Book Tuesday or Wednesday departures for the lowest fares.

✈️ Recommended: Book ANA or JAL from LAX/SFO to Haneda (HND). Haneda is only ~30 min from central Tokyo vs. ~60 min for Narita. Aim for an afternoon arrival on Aug 1 so you can pick up the rental car and reach Atami by evening.

🪪 Driving in Japan: You need an International Driving Permit (IDP) based on the 1949 Geneva Convention. Get it from AAA ($20) before you leave the US. Japan drives on the left. Most road signs are bilingual (Japanese/English). Google Maps works great for navigation.

Day 3 — Mon Aug 3: MOA Museum & Izusan Fireworks Festival 🎆 FIREWORKS
MOA Museum — world-class art overlooking the sea
The Izusan fireworks light up Atami Bay
🗺️ View Route: Museum → Fireworks →
🎆 Izusan Onsen Marine Fireworks Festival — The 75th edition! Known as the "matchmaking fireworks" (縁結び花火). ~2,000 shots including rapid-fire star mines and a gold-and-silver Niagara finale. Held rain or shine. Time: 20:20–20:45. Free viewing.
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–10:00🥐 Leisurely BreakfastSleep in a bit — it's vacation! Breakfast at your Airbnb.
10:00–12:30🎨 MOA Museum of ArtWorld-class collection of Japanese and East Asian art. The museum itself is a modernist masterpiece by architect Kikutake Kiyonori, with a stunning escalator entrance and panoramic ocean views. ¥1,600/adult, students ¥1,000. Highlights: Ogata Korin's Red and White Plum Blossoms screen.
12:30–14:00🍵 Lunch at MOAThe museum café has incredible ocean views. Try the matcha and Japanese sweets set.
14:00–16:00🏖️ Afternoon Beach TimeRelax at Atami Sun Beach or explore the nearby Akao Herb & Rose Garden (¥1,200) for flower fields and ocean vistas.
16:00–17:30🚿 Rest & PrepareHead back to freshen up. Pick up snacks, drinks, and a picnic blanket from a konbini for the fireworks.
17:30–18:30🍣 Early DinnerEat early — restaurants near the fireworks viewing area will be packed. Grab ekiben (station bento) or convenience store feast for a beach picnic.
18:30–19:30🎆 Stake Out SpotWalk to the Izusan Coast viewing area (15–20 min). Arrive 1+ hour early for a good spot. Bring a beach mat or blanket.
20:20–20:45🎆 IZUSAN FIREWORKS!The grand show — fireworks reflected in the bay, echoing off the mountains. The natural amphitheater effect makes these some of Japan's most dramatic fireworks.
21:00–21:30🚶 Walk BackExpect crowds leaving. Walk slowly, soak in the festive atmosphere. Grab a cold drink at a vending machine.

Crystal-clear Izu waters

Day 5 — Wed Aug 5: Mt. Omuro, Zoo & Atami Marine Fireworks 🎆 FIREWORKS
Mt. Omuro — Izu's iconic "matcha bowl" volcano
Capybaras and cactus at Izu Shaboten Zoo
🗺️ View Route: Mt. Omuro → Zoo → Atami Fireworks →
🎆 Atami Marine Fireworks Festival — The famous summer series! Tonight's show launches from Atami Bay. The mountains create a natural amphitheater, making the sound BOOM like nowhere else. The "Niagara in the Sky" finale is legendary. Time: 20:20–20:45. Only a 30-min drive back to Atami from Ito!
TimeActivityDetails
8:30–9:00🥐 Early BreakfastQuick breakfast — Mt. Omuro gets busy by late morning.
9:00–11:00🌋 Mt. OmuroTake the chairlift (¥700/adult, ¥400/child) to the 580m summit. Walk the 1km crater rim for 360° panoramic views — Mt. Fuji to the north, Izu Islands to the south. On clear summer mornings, this is THE spot for Fuji views. The crater itself is an archery range (you can try kyudo!).
11:00–12:00🌵 Izu Shaboten ZooRight next to Mt. Omuro. Famous for its free-roaming capybaras (yes, you can pet them) and cactus greenhouse. The teens will love the quirky animal interactions. ¥2,400/adult, ¥1,200/child.
12:00–13:00🍜 LunchEat at the zoo café or a nearby soba shop. Try wasabi soba — Izu is Japan's wasabi capital.
13:00–14:30🏔️ Komuroyama (optional)If Omuro is cloudy (summer haze), try Komuroyama nearby — a smaller peak with a single-person cable car (¥500 one-way) and great views.
14:30–17:00🚿 Rest & BeachRest at your Airbnb or hit Ito Orange Beach for a swim.
17:00–18:30🚗 Drive to Atami~30 min drive back north. Park early — fireworks nights bring traffic. Aim to arrive by 18:30 to find parking.
18:30–19:30🍣 Early DinnerGrab food near Atami Station or bring a picnic to the beach.
19:30–20:20🏖️ Find Viewing SpotAtami Sun Beach or Atami Kaihin Park. The beach fills up — stake your spot early.
20:20–20:45🎆 ATAMI MARINE FIREWORKS!The iconic "Niagara in the Sky" — a curtain of fireworks cascading across the entire bay. With the mountain backdrop, this is one of Japan's best summer fireworks experiences.
21:00–21:30🚗 Drive back to ItoTraffic will be heavy leaving. Take it slow, or grab a late-night ramen in Atami while the crowds disperse.
Day 6 — Thu Aug 6: Kawazu Seven Waterfalls & Wasabi Fields
Kawazu Nanadaru — seven falls in a lush gorge
The cool, green interior of Izu's mountains
🗺️ View Route: Ito → Waterfalls → Joren Falls →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–10:00🚗 Drive to Kawazu~1 hour south from Ito along the coastal highway. Beautiful drive with ocean on your left.
10:00–10:30🏠 Drop BagsCheck into your Kawazu Airbnb or leave luggage.
10:30–12:30💧 Kawazu Seven WaterfallsWalk the 1.5 km trail along the Kawazu River through a basalt gorge. Seven distinct waterfalls — each with its own character. Bronze statues from The Izu Dancer line the path. The double-spiral Kawazu Nanadaru Loop Bridge (45m diameter!) is an engineering marvel you'll pass on the drive up. Free entry.
12:30–13:30🍱 LunchRiverside soba shop near the falls. Try wasabi-don (fresh wasabi over rice with bonito flakes) — the wasabi here is the real deal, grown in mountain spring water.
13:30–14:30🌿 Joren FallsA stunning 25m waterfall plunging into a deep blue pool. The light filtering through the forest creates an almost magical atmosphere. Great photo spot. You can try trout fishing here — they'll salt-grill your catch!
14:30–16:00🌱 Wasabi FieldsVisit the terraced wasabi fields near the waterfalls. The Amagi area produces some of Japan's finest wasabi. Many farms have small shops where you can buy fresh wasabi root (great souvenir) and wasabi-flavored snacks — wasabi ice cream is a must-try!
16:00–18:00🏠 Settle InHead to your Airbnb. Kawazu is quieter — enjoy the mountain-valley peace.
18:00–20:00🍲 DinnerKawazu has excellent little family-run restaurants. Try a wasabi shabu-shabu if you can find it — thinly sliced pork in wasabi-infused broth.
Day 7 — Fri Aug 7: Shimoda — Beaches, Black Ships & Aquarium
Shirahama Beach — Izu's crown jewel
Historic Perry Road — where Japan opened to the world
🗺️ View Route: Kawazu → Aquarium → Perry Road → Iritahama →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–9:30🚗 Drive to Shimoda~30 min south from Kawazu. Easy coastal drive.
9:30–10:00🏠 Drop BagsCheck into your Shimoda Airbnb near Shirahama or Iritahama Beach.
10:00–12:00🐬 Shimoda AquariumSmall but excellent aquarium. The highlight: dolphin swimming programs (book ahead) and the underwater tunnel. Great for families. ¥2,100/adult, ¥1,050/child.
12:00–13:30🍕 Lunch at IritahamaHead to FermenCo. at Iritahama Beach — a wood-fired sourdough pizzeria right on the sand. Try the quattro formaggi with local honey. One of the most memorable meals of your trip.
13:30–15:30⛵ Perry Road & HistoryWalk along the willow-lined canal where Commodore Perry walked when he opened Japan to the world in 1854. Visit the Ryosenji Temple where the Treaty of Kanagawa was signed. The Shimoda History Museum tells the fascinating Black Ships story.
15:30–17:30🏖️ Iritahama BeachOften called one of Japan's most beautiful beaches. White sand, blue water, less crowded than Shirahama. Perfect for a late-afternoon swim.
17:30–19:00🚿 Freshen UpShower at your Airbnb. Sunset walk on the beach if you're up for it.
19:00–21:00🐟 Seafood DinnerTsuji — Shimoda's legendary kinmedai restaurant (reservations recommended). Their braised golden eye snapper is life-changing. The teens will love it — the fish is sweet, tender, and served in a rich soy-mirin broth. Budget ~¥5,000/person for the full course.
Day 8 — Sat Aug 8: Shirahama Beach & Cape Irozaki
Shirahama's AAA-rated crystal waters
Cape Irozaki — where the Izu Peninsula ends
🗺️ View Route: Shirahama → Sea Cave → Cape Irozaki →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–9:30🥐 BreakfastPastries and coffee at Tofuya Bakery & Table — a stylish café with a foot-bath terrace (ashiyu) where you soak your feet while eating. Pure Izu bliss.
9:30–13:00🏖️ Shirahama BeachThe crown jewel of Izu beaches. AAA-rated water quality, white sand stretching 800m. The red torii gate at the north end makes it one of Japan's most photogenic beaches. Rent umbrellas and beach chairs (~¥2,000/set). Swim, snorkel, or just float in the crystal-clear water.
13:00–14:00🍤 Beach LunchBeachside cafés and food stalls. The ika-yaki (grilled squid on a stick) from the beach vendors is a must.
14:00–15:00🕳️ Ryugu Sea CaveDrive 10 min to the natural sea cave. The opening is heart-shaped when viewed from above — a viral Instagram spot. You can take a boat ride through the cave (~¥1,500/person).
15:00–17:00🗼 Cape IrozakiThe southernmost tip of the Izu Peninsula. Dramatic cliffs, a white lighthouse, and views stretching to the Izu Islands. The coastal trail along the cape is stunning in late-afternoon light. Great whale-watching potential in summer.
17:00–19:00🚿 Rest & RelaxHead back to the Airbnb. If your rental has a BBQ, tonight's the night for a beachside grill session.
19:00–21:00🍣 Farewell Shimoda DinnerTry a different seafood spot, or go casual at an izakaya near Shimoda Station. Order ise-ebi (Japanese spiny lobster) if it's in season.
💡 Beach Day Tip: Shirahama gets busy on weekends (today is Saturday). Arrive before 9:30 to claim a good spot. The left side near the torii gate tends to be slightly less crowded. Water shoes recommended — some areas have small rocks.
Day 9 — Sun Aug 9: West Coast Drive — Toi Gold Mine & Dogashima Sunset
Dogashima's dramatic Sunset Coast
Toi Gold Mine — walk through 400-year-old tunnels
🗺️ View Route: Shimoda → Toi → Dogashima →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–10:30🚗 Scenic Drive~1.5 hours from Shimoda to the west coast. This is one of Japan's most beautiful coastal drives — winding roads with the Pacific on your right. Stop at Koganezaki viewpoint for a photo break.
10:30–12:00⛏️ Toi Gold MineWalk through 400-year-old mining tunnels — once the second most productive gold mine in Japan. The tunnels are cool (great on a hot August day) and the history is fascinating. Try gold-flake soft serve at the café! ¥860/adult, ¥430/child.
12:00–13:00🍜 Lunch in ToiSmall seafood restaurants along the port. The shirasu-don (baby sardines over rice) is a local specialty.
13:00–14:00🚗 Coastal DriveContinue south along the Sunset Coast. Stop at viewpoints — the contrast of green mountains and blue sea is incredible.
14:00–15:00🏠 Settle InCheck into your Dogashima Airbnb. West coast rentals are more rustic and secluded — enjoy the peace.
15:00–17:00🌊 Dogashima CoastExplore the dramatic rock formations and sea-sculpted coastline. Tomboro — a natural rock arch — is the signature sight. Walk the seaside trails for endless photo ops.
17:00–18:30🌅 Sunset at DogashimaThis is the "Sunset Coast" for a reason. Find a spot overlooking the bay as the sun sinks into Suruga Bay behind Mt. Fuji's silhouette. The golden light on the rock formations is pure magic. Top photo of the trip.
18:30–20:00🍲 DinnerWest coast dining is more limited — your Airbnb kitchen is your best friend. Pick up fresh seafood from a local market and cook a feast. Or find a small local ryori-ryokan serving kaiseki dinner.
Day 10 — Mon Aug 10: Sea Caves, Matsuzaki Old Town & Coastal Relaxation
Dogashima sea cave boat tours
Matsuzaki's preserved Edo-era streets
🗺️ View Route: Dogashima → Matsuzaki →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–10:30🚤 Sea Cave Boat TourTake a 20-min boat cruise exploring the sea caves and rock formations around Dogashima. The Tensodo Cave — where sunlight pierces through a hole in the cave ceiling — is the highlight. Boats depart regularly from Dogashima port. ¥1,500/adult, ¥750/child.
10:30–11:30🌊 Cape Ose (optional)If you're feeling adventurous, drive to Cape Ose — a remote beach where you can see Mt. Fuji from the sand. The road is winding and narrow but the reward is worth it. One of the few beaches in Japan with full Fuji views.
11:30–12:00🚗 Drive to Matsuzaki~20 min north along the coast.
12:00–13:00🍜 Lunch in MatsuzakiMatsuzaki is famous for sakura-ebi (cherry blossom shrimp) from Suruga Bay. Try the sakura-ebi kakiage (shrimp tempura fritter over rice) at a local restaurant.
13:00–15:00🏘️ Matsuzaki Old TownStroll through the Namako Wall district — Edo-period merchant houses with distinctive black-and-white lattice walls. It's like stepping back 200 years with virtually no crowds (unlike Kyoto). Visit the Iwashina School, Japan's oldest elementary school building — a beautiful Meiji-era wooden structure.
15:00–17:00🏖️ Matsuzaki BeachA quieter local beach. Swim, read, or just enjoy the pace of small-town coastal Japan.
17:00–19:00🚗 Return & RelaxDrive back to Dogashima. Last sunset-watching session from your west coast perch.
19:00–20:30🍲 Final West Coast DinnerAnother home-cooked feast or a local restaurant. Savor the tranquility — tomorrow you head inland.
Day 11 — Tue Aug 11: Shuzenji Onsen — Temples, Bamboo & Foot Baths
Shuzenji Temple — 1,200 years of history
Shuzenji's magical bamboo forest path
🗺️ View Route: Dogashima → Shuzenji →
TimeActivityDetails
9:00–10:00🚗 Drive to Shuzenji~1 hour drive inland from Dogashima through mountain roads. The landscape changes from coastal to lush forest.
10:00–10:30🏠 Settle InCheck into your Shuzenji Airbnb. This is your onsen retreat — hopefully with a private hot spring bath!
10:30–12:00⛩️ Shuzenji TempleThe heart of this 1,200-year-old hot spring town. Founded by Kobo Daishi (the same monk who founded the Shikoku pilgrimage). The temple grounds are serene with ancient cedars and a thatched-roof hall. Free entry.
12:00–13:30🍜 LunchTry onsen manju (steamed buns made with hot spring water) from a street vendor, then proper lunch at a soba or unagi (eel) restaurant along the main street.
13:30–15:00🎋 Bamboo Forest PathShuzenji's bamboo grove rivals Kyoto's Arashiyama — but with a fraction of the crowds. Walk the lantern-lined path through towering bamboo. The light filtering through the stalks is mesmerizing. Perfect for family photos.
15:00–16:30♨️ Foot Baths & Kaede BridgeSoak your feet in the free ashiyu (foot baths) along the Katsura River. The red Kaede Bridge over the river is the town's most iconic view. Legend says couples who cross it together find eternal love (the teens will cringe, you'll love it).
16:30–18:00♨️ Tokko-no-YuThe original hot spring that started it all — a simple wooden bathhouse on the river where Kobo Daishi is said to have bathed 1,200 years ago. The water is alkaline, leaving your skin impossibly smooth. ¥350/adult. Or save the full onsen experience for tomorrow.
18:00–20:00🍲 DinnerShuzenji has excellent kaiseki (multi-course Japanese dinner) restaurants. Book a meal at your Airbnb if the host offers it, or try Yumeguri no Yado Katsuragawa for a restaurant dinner with private onsen access (~¥10,000/person with onsen and meal).
♨️ Onsen Etiquette: Wash thoroughly before entering the bath. No swimsuits — Japanese onsen are nude (but gender-separated). Tattoos may be an issue at some public baths — check in advance. Private/family baths (kashikiri-buro) are a great option if anyone has tattoos or prefers privacy.
Day 12 — Wed Aug 12: Shuzenji Relaxation — Onsen, Amagi Tunnel & Deep Rest
A day dedicated to the art of hot spring bathing
Old Amagi Tunnel — cool, mysterious, and moss-covered
🗺️ View Route: Shuzenji → Amagi Tunnel →

This is your designated recharge day. After 11 days of exploration, everyone needs a breather — especially the teens. Sleep in, soak, and take it slow.

🗺️ Driving & Navigation Tips

🚗 Essential Driving Info:
• Japan drives on the LEFT (same as UK, opposite of US)
• Speed limits: 40-50 km/h in towns, 80-100 km/h on expressways (everyone goes slightly faster)
• Most road signs are bilingual (Japanese + English)
• Google Maps works great for navigation — download offline maps as backup
• Expressway rest stops (SA/PA) in Japan are amazing — clean bathrooms, great food courts, local souvenirs
• Gas stations are full-service — just say "mantan" for a full tank
• ETC card is essential — Japan's toll system is entirely electronic. Your rental car company will provide one

💴 Money & Connectivity

• Cash is still king in rural Izu — many small restaurants and attractions are cash-only. Carry ¥30,000–50,000 at all times
• 7-Eleven ATMs (Seven Bank) accept foreign cards and are everywhere
• SIM cards: Rent a pocket Wi-Fi or buy a data SIM at the airport. Izu coverage is good but can be spotty in mountain areas
• Suica/IC cards: Get one for each family member for trains, konbini purchases, and some vending machines
• Tipping does not exist in Japan. Exceptional service is the baseline

🏥 Safety & Health

• Japan is one of the safest countries in the world — violent crime is virtually nonexistent
• August is hot and humid (30-35°C / 86-95°F with high humidity). Stay hydrated, wear sun protection
• Mosquitoes can be active near rivers and waterfalls — bring repellent
• Tap water is safe to drink everywhere
• Travel insurance is strongly recommended — Japan's healthcare is excellent but expensive for uninsured visitors
• Convenience stores (konbini) sell everything you'd need in a pinch — from underwear to phone chargers to surprisingly good food

🌸 August in Izu — What to Expect

• Peak summer: Hot and humid. Beaches are at their best — water temperature ~26°C (79°F)
• Obon holiday (Aug 8–17): Japan's summer family travel period. Expect more domestic tourists at popular spots. Book everything early
• Fireworks: Multiple fireworks festivals during your stay — you're perfectly timed for the Izusan (Aug 3), Atami Marine (Aug 5), and Taga Bay (Aug 12) shows
• Crowds: Beaches are busiest on weekends (Aug 1-2, 8-9). Plan beach days for weekdays when possible
• Typhoon possibility: August is typhoon season. Rare but possible — monitor weather forecasts. Have a flexible indoor backup plan for 1-2 days
